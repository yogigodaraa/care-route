# Distribution & Reach Strategy — "Where to Go When"

## The Problem
We have the solution built. Now: how do people actually discover it and use it?

---

## Channel 1: Google Search (Highest Intent)
- People Google things like "should I go to ER for chest pain", "urgent care near me open now", "child fever when to go to hospital"
- **SEO play:** Create landing pages targeting these searches that funnel into our tool
- **Google Ads:** Target health-panic search terms — cost per click is worth it if we can prove diversion value
- This is the #1 channel because it catches people *at the moment of decision*

## Channel 2: Embed Into GP & Clinic Websites
- GPs benefit from fewer after-hours calls and better patient flow
- Offer a free embeddable widget for GP clinic websites
- When patients visit their GP's website after hours, they hit our tool instead of going to ED
- **Pitch:** "Reduce your after-hours call volume by giving patients a smart self-triage tool on your website"

## Channel 3: Partner With Platforms People Already Use
- **HotDoc / HealthEngine** — patients already book GP appointments here, integrate there
- **Private health insurers** — they save money when members avoid ED, strong incentive to promote our tool
- **Pharmacy chains** (Chemist Warehouse, Priceline) — in-store QR codes, pharmacy apps

## Channel 4: Government & Institutional Partnerships
- State health departments already run "don't go to ED" campaigns — we're the tool behind the campaign
- Hospital ED waiting rooms — QR code: "Next time, check here first" (captures them for next time)
- Medicare/MyGov integration

## Channel 5: Community & Word of Mouth
- **Parents groups** — Facebook groups, mothers groups, daycare newsletters (parents are the #1 audience)
- **New resident packs** — councils send info to new residents, include our tool
- **Workplace health programs** — employers embed in intranet/benefits portal

## Channel 6: Proactive / Push-Based
- **SMS/notification before peak times** — e.g., Friday evening: "Feeling unwell tonight? Check where to go before heading to ED"
- **Weather/flu season triggers** — when flu season peaks, push awareness through partners
- **GP visit follow-up** — after a GP visit, patient gets a link: "Next time you're unsure, try this first"

---

## Key Principle
Don't ask people to download an app or remember a URL. 
Be embedded in the places they already go, at the moments they're making the decision.

---

## Open Questions

- How do we measure success (diverted ED visits, tool usage, partner adoption)?
- What's the pitch deck for GP clinics / insurers / government?
- How do we handle trust — what makes someone believe our tool over just going to ED?
- After-hours is peak ED time and GPs are closed — how do we position for that moment?
